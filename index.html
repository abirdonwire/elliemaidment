About Me

History

I originally trained as journalist, but my heart just wasn't in it.
I didn't yet know what it was that I wanted to do so I got a job working in the Advertising department at a publisher of Food and Drink magazines, William Reed Business Media. I learned a lot about company dynamics and processes before moving onto Journalism.co.uk.

It was here that I wrote my first lines of HTML and became interested in all things web-related.
In 2009, I was made redundant after the big economic crash in 2008 but managed to find myself a role as an Account Manager at ResponseSource, a leading SaaS supplier for the media and communications sector. There were lots more opportunities to learn about tech, but I suffered from a huge lack of confidence so I kept to my role, even though I was desperate to discover more from the development team.
It was only after leaving ResponseSource at the end of my maternity leave that I finally had the determination to learn to how to code. I had the time to be able to dedicate to learning at my own pace and I had nothing to lose. One of my proudest moments was when I deployed my first ever website that I designed and developed myself from scratch.

I'm proud to say that I am 100% self taught.

It hasn't always been easy, but's been totally worth it.

My Return to Tech

Technology is endlessly fascinating and constantly evolving.
It's impossible to get bored, there is always a new problem solve or a new development to discover and I find it so exciting.
I want to return to the tech sector so I can use my new skills, and combine them with my existing experience.

Since learning to code, I am also a passionate advocate for women in technology. I am open about my own experience in the hope that it will inspire others to learn to code too. I think that the more women there are visibly working in technology the better.

I know I have a lot to offer the industry and I'm absolutely delighted to have this opportunity.
